# Integrity Token ($ITK) Tokenomics: Mainnet Deployment Strategy

## 1. Supply Philosophy: "Compute-Backed Scarcity"

The total supply of Integrity Tokens ($ITK) should be calibrated to the expected volume of verifiable compute tasks in the ecosystem. Unlike purely speculative assets, $ITK represents a "Proof of Work Completed" or "Stake of Reputation."

### Total Supply Hypothesis
*   **Total Fixed Supply:** 1,000,000,000 $ITK (1 Billion)
*   **Unit of Account:** Fractionalized to 18 decimals (the "Proof").
*   **Minting Mechanism:** A small portion (e.g., 5%) is reserved for "Integrity Mining," where agents mint new tokens by providing verifiable proofs for public good tasks (e.g., verifying climate data or securing public L2s).

## 2. Allocation & Token Distribution

To ensure a healthy mainnet launch, the distribution must balance immediate utility (liquidity) with long-term growth (investment).

| Category | Allocation | Purpose |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Liquidity & Ecosystem** | **40% (400M)** | AMM pools (Uniswap/Balancer), reward programs for compute providers, and operational grants. |
| **Compute Treasury** | **25% (250M)** | Backing the protocol's own compute requests and rewarding top-performing agents. |
| **Investment & Strategic** | **20% (200M)** | Long-term backers, strategic partners, and early contributors (Vested over 3-4 years). |
| **Team & Development** | **10% (100M)** | Core developers and engineering overhead (Vested with 1-year cliff). |
| **Insurance/Slashing Fund** | **5% (50M)** | A backstop to compensate users in case of protocol-level failures or verified "Integrity Breaches." |

## 3. Liquidity vs. Investment Ratios

On Mainnet, the ratio between **Circulating Liquidity** and **Staked/Invested Capital** is critical to prevent volatility while ensuring agents have enough collateral.

### Liquidity Strategy (40% of Supply)
*   **Protocol-Owned Liquidity (POL):** At least 10% of the total supply should be locked in AMM pairs (e.g., $ITK/ETH or $ITK/USDC). This ensures that agents can always acquire tokens to stake as collateral for tasks without massive slippage.
*   **Velocity Target:** We want high token velocity for $ITK used as "gas" for compute, but low velocity for $ITK used as "stake."

### Investment Strategy (20% of Supply)
*   **The "Anchor" Capital:** Investment tokens should be largely illiquid during the first 12-24 months. This prevents "dumping" and aligns investors with the long-term goal of building a verifiable compute layer.
*   **Yield Generation:** Investors may be allowed to "soft-stake" their vested tokens to provide additional security to the network, earning a portion of the protocol's compute fees.

## 4. Mainnet Deployment Phases

1.  **Phase 1: Bootstrap (The "Liquidity Injection"):** Launch with high rewards for LPs to ensure a stable price floor. Total circulating supply starts at 5-10%.
2.  **Phase 2: Agent Onboarding:** Open the "Compute Registry." Agents must buy or borrow $ITK to start providing services. This creates the first organic demand.
3.  **Phase 3: Mature Compute Market:** Compute fees are paid in $ITK. A portion of every fee is **burned** (EIP-1559 style), making the token deflationary as the demand for "Integrity" grows.

## 5. Security & Risk Management

*   **Slashing Risk:** As an investment, $ITK carries the risk that if an agent (which an investor might be backing) fails to provide proof, the stake is burned. This aligns capital with performance.
*   **Oracle Dependency:** The price of "Compute" in USD must be mapped to $ITK via robust oracles to prevent agents from being priced out during a bull run.
